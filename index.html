Answers to Common Questions

Salary & Payments

Payroll is processed between 6th and 9th of each month.

Funds are credited into your nominated dollar account in AUD. We will provide you with a recipient created tax invoice (RCTI) to meet your compliance requirements.

What is the leave request process?

  1. You need to gain approval from your AKQA direct report before applying for leave
  2. Once granted, please apply via BetterHR and state that you have prior approval from your line manager

What if I have a HR related question?

For any HR-related inquiries, including benefits, leave entitlements, and policies, please reach out to the Maytech HR Manager, who will be your primary point of contact.

Whom should I contact regarding general work-related matters?

Your primary point of contact for work-related queries, concerns, and suggestions is the Director of Engineering and Technology. They will serve as your main resource for addressing or escalating any issues related to your tasks.

If the outcome is unsatisfactory, you may escalate the matter to James Freeman, the Managing Partner of Maytech Holdings.

Do I need to check in and out daily on BetterHR?

Yes, regardless of whether you are in the office or remote, you are required to check in/out via the BetterHR application.

Leave & Attendance - Event Based

Paid Maternity Leave

  • First and Second Child: 84 days of paid leave (14 days before confinement and 70 days after)
  • Third and Subsequent Child: 42 days of paid leave (14 days before confinement and 28 days after)
  • Eligibility: Only available for Confirmed Employees
  • Exclusive of weekly holidays, public holidays, and Poya holidays
  • Application to be made 12 weeks prior to expected date

Paid Paternity Leave

  • 3 days of Paid Leave
  • Eligibility: Only available for Confirmed Employees
  • Must be taken within 30 days of the child's birth
  • Application to be made 12 weeks prior to expected date

Orion City Security Requirements

When to Wear Your Lanyard

Your Maytech ID and lanyard must be visibly worn:

  • Commuting to Work: From entering the building complex until reaching your office space
  • Commuting from Work: From leaving your office space until exiting the building complex
  • On-Site Facilities: When using restrooms, cafeterias, break rooms, and other shared spaces

Note: Once you have arrived at the office, you may remove your lanyard. This is a security requirement mandated by Orion City.

Interacting in the Workplace

Client Confidentiality - Foundations

As you've all signed Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) with Maytech, it's crucial to remember that these agreements extend to all client interactions.

Information Sensitivity

  • All client information is considered confidential unless explicitly designated otherwise
  • Examples include: client financial data, strategic plans, customer lists, internal communications, personal matters, intellectual property, codebase repositories

Data Security

  • Handle all client data with utmost care and security
  • Use company-issued devices only
  • Adhere to all data security protocols

Confidentiality in Practice

Communication Channels

  • Use appropriate communication channels (company-approved email, secure messaging platforms like Slack)
  • Avoid discussing confidential information in public areas or on personal devices
  • Be mindful of information shared on client internal communication platforms

Data Storage

  • Store all client data securely on company servers or designated secure locations
  • Do not store sensitive information on personal devices or cloud storage accounts
  • Follow proper file-naming and storage conventions

Meetings

  • Maintain confidentiality during all client meetings, both in-person and virtual
  • Avoid discussing sensitive information in hallways or break rooms
  • Be mindful of who is present in meeting rooms or common office areas

Simply: Let's keep client confidences between us

Maintaining Neutrality and Professionalism

Let's Do a U-Turn From These:

  • Avoid getting drawn into office gossip, rumors, or internal debates
  • Stay out of arguments or disagreements between client colleagues
  • Avoid taking sides in internal conflicts
  • Do not participate in internal client surveys or petitions unless specifically directed

Let's Focus on These:

  • Maintain a neutral and professional stance
  • Focus on de-escalation when observing conflicts
  • Communicate respectfully and professionally
  • Remember your actions reflect on both you and Maytech

Deliver excellence, avoid distractions!
